The aim of most patients suffering from lower back pain is not just to be sure of enjoying pain-free movement again but to forestall future occurrence of the back pain. Lower back pain can be deteriorating and depressing if not treated correctly.

Your Lower Back

There are five vertebrae present in the lower back or ‘lumbar spine,’ with cartilage discs in between to buffer them. The protective cage that covers the spinal cord is formed from the vertebrae. The sacrum sees the end of your lumbar spine, that portion that rams into your pelvis. The stability of your spine depends on the ligaments and muscles of the back and the abdomen. There is increasing evident to suggest core stability is vital in the rehabilitation of low back pain.

Lower Back Pain Causes

Lots of various causes can be linked to lower back pains, each person will present differently. Therefore, treatment are individualized.
Patients can sometime report a trauma of some sorts, where you were really hurt as the start of the back pain. However, most commonly it is slowly progressing with no obvious injury.

Conventional sources of Lower Back Pain

Back Muscle Strains A back muscle strain can happen for many reasons. Carrying weighty objects; wrong lifting postures and muscle burnout can result in poor joint stability and injury. Leaning from the waist instead of the knees to pick heavy objects is one of the most common precursors of backaches.
Ligament Sprains A ligament sprain occurs when the ligament fibres are torn. Ligaments are solid fibrous bands and are very tough; however, if they are strained much further than they can bear, they get torn. The aftermath of this is swelling and severe pain, which is the after effect of a car accident or some other vicious trauma.
Poor Posture

Drooping while in a sitting position or standing and lifting things stresses your spine. Muscles weakness, ligaments strains, and discs stretch can cause your spinal joints and nerves to be under intense pressure.

There’s a plethora of list that is distinct to lower back pain causes, from osteoporosis to degenerative disc disease, however, a significant therapy in tackling the pain is a custom-made program of lower back physiotherapy treatment.

Lower Back Pain Treatment

A host of lower back pains can be remedied by physiotherapy treatment, granting you sweet freedom from pains. It’s essential to have an accurate early assessment because the injury never gets better with the days if urgent measures are not taking on time.

The special treatment will be different based on a particular diagnosis; however, plenty of options are available to help you. Pain management will be the first therapy to tackling inflammation and swelling, so the present trauma in that area is fixed. Depending on your kind of injury, dry needling and electrical stimulation or therapeutic massage and traction can come in handy to address it. Irrespective of your treatment plan, your DC Physiotherapists in our Physio Clinic Clondalkin/Lucan will work with you every step of the way.

Back Pain Treatment Swellings can be assuaged by a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory pill same with cold or heat therapy (depending on what stage your injury is). Your doctor might prescribe some painkillers for you, but the primal aim is to heal the injury and free yourself from depending on pharmaceuticals.
Restoring Normal Range of Motion and Stretch

As the inflammation pacifies, the level of your pains will become better, and your DC Physiotherapist will now switch to ensuring normalcy of your lumbar spine function to allow you to move and bend. The focus will be on the muscles around the spines identified as core muscles which form a shield around the spine to ensure stress is appropriately shared.

Fine abdominal muscles are helpful in providing a base to your lower back, so base on fitness level and distinct case, your physiotherapist will formulate a core stability program for you. Ideally, Pilates is great to gradually mold core strength to protect against re injuring your lower back.

Restoring Full Function

You and your physiotherapist will work as a team to restore your lower back in shape so that you can resume your normal life. Different strokes for different folks when it comes to lower back- some folks are stuck to their desk all day, so your physiotherapists will offer helpful tips and hints on how to reduce strain on your back, like observing some dips and stretches for some minutes each day. They’ll counsel you on the appropriate sitting posture too. Sportsmen will be stressing their lower spine, so this will decide how your rehabilitation plan will go.

How do you stop it from happening again?

A physiotherapy assessment will identify the root cause of the problem, treatment may include tackled and remodelled any lifestyle or habits that are precursors to your back pain. Two primal physical components that everyone hinges on to maintain a healthy lower back:

  1. A back that is fluid and moves freely!
  2. A well-conditioned back that sees the core musculature absorbing the bulk of the load being heaped on the back instead of the structures that make up your spines such as the discs and intervertebral joints.

Adherence to Physiotherapy rehabilitation is vital in your recovery. The possibilities of reoccurring pain is high if you don’t complete your rehabilitation program, disobeying the rules in building up core abdominal strength and sustaining mobility is often held culpable for re-occurring back pain!

Physiotherapy rehabilitation should be completed a few times in a week. A program will be created by your physiotherapist that you can comfortably do at home; it’s Imperative that you adhere strictly to these as it builds up energy and flexibility to help guard against another injury.

With the proper lower back pain exercises and special treatment, and of course a guide from your physiotherapist, you can finally arrive at that point where you’re free from pain.
